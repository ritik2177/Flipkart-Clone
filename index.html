About:

Flipkart, founded in 2007 by Sachin and Binny Bansal, is one of India's leading e-commerce platforms. Originally started as an online bookstore, it quickly diversified its offerings to include a wide range of products such as electronics, fashion, home appliances, and groceries. Headquartered in Bengaluru, Flipkart has played a pivotal role in shaping India's e-commerce landscape, pioneering the concept of online shopping.
One of Flipkart's key strategies has been its focus on understanding and catering to the unique needs of Indian consumers. This includes offering cash-on-delivery payment options, easy returns, and delivery services to even the remotest parts of the country. The company also invests heavily in logistics and supply chain management, ensuring that products are delivered efficiently across India. Its logistics arm, Ekart, has been instrumental in this regard, providing reliable delivery services that meet the high expectations of consumers .
In addition to its vast product catalog, Flipkart has also developed its own range of private-label brands across various categories, offering consumers a broader selection at competitive prices. In 2018, Flipkart made headlines when Walmart, the American retail giant, acquired a 77% stake in the company for $16 billion, marking one of the largest e-commerce deals globally. This acquisition provided Flipkart with the financial muscle to compete with other e-commerce giants like Amazon, which has been rapidly expanding in India.
Flipkart is also known for its Big Billion Days sale, an annual event that offers massive discounts and promotions across all product categories, attracting millions of customers. The sale has become one of the most anticipated shopping events in India, contributing significantly to the company's sales and revenue.
